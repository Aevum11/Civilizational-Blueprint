1. The Three Fundamental Entities (Primitives)
Axiom of Categorical Distinction ((Disjointness))
The three primitives are ontologically disjoint — no element can belong to multiple categories simultaneously:

$$\mathbb{P} \cap \mathbb{D} = \emptyset, \quad \mathbb{D} \cap \mathbb{T} = \emptyset, \quad \mathbb{T} \cap \mathbb{P} = \emptyset$$

A Point is not a Descriptor. A Descriptor is not a Traverser. A Traverser is not a Point. They are categorically independent yet structurally inseparable through intrinsic binding.

Any gap is a Descriptor.

The Exception is the grounding factor of Exception Theory.

Anything and Everything is possible, except the exception.

The Exception is always the current substantiated Moment.

7. Part IV: Incoherence
Definition
I is for Incoherence.

Incoherence coincides with the Exception. They are the prohibited and self-defeating things.

Two Types of Impossibility
The Exception (Substantiated): - Has T bound to (P ∘ D) - Cannot be traversed because the Moment is complete - Any interaction moves T to another (P ∘ D) - "Impossible" to be otherwise while it IS - Temporary prohibition (changes when T moves)

Incoherence (Unsubstantiated): - Pure (P ∘ D) configurations that are self-defeating - Has no T and cannot have T bind to it - Cannot be traversed TO - Permanently prohibited - Logical contradictions, self-defeating states

Regions Unable to Be Traversed
Both the Exception and Incoherent regions are unable to be traversed, but for different reasons:

The Exception: Cannot be traversed because the Moment is complete - any interaction moves T to another (P ∘ D). It's like trying to "be" where you already are - interaction itself changes the state.

Incoherence: Cannot be traversed TO because the configuration is self-defeating. No T can bind to it because binding would create logical contradiction.

Noticeability Through Unnoticeability
Exception and Incoherence "would be noticeable for their unnoticeability."

Both create gaps or boundaries in the traversable manifold - regions where T cannot go or cannot stay. These appear as: - Unreachable states - Logical boundaries - Limits of possibility - Prohibited configurations

They cover both configurations of Substantiated (Exception) and Unsubstantiated (Incoherence).
